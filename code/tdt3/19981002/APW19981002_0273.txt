Aiko Takasu and Mayumi Murai each shot a 3-under-par 69 Friday and 
shared a one-shot lead after the first round of the 60 million yen 
(dlrs 441,000) Kosaido Ladies Golf Cup. Takasu sank four birdies against 
one bogey on the 6,257-yard, par-72 Chiba Kosaido Country Club course. 
Murai, a six-time winner on the Japan LPGA tour, had five birdies 
and two bogeys. The veteran Takasu won the Yakult Ladies golf tournament 
in May. South Korea's Ku Ok-hee sank three birdies on the back nine 
and shared second place at 70 with Nayoko Yoshikawa. Ku won the Chukyo 
TV Bridgestone tournament in May. Four golfers, including Lee Oh-soon 
and Han Hee-won of South Korea, were tied at 71, two strokes behind 
the leaders. A total of 108 golfers teed off in the 54-hole event. 
The winner receives 10.8 million yen (dlrs 80,000).
