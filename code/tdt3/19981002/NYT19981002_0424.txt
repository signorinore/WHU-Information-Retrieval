Chester Lasell, a former president of Morgan Stanley &AMP; Co., the 
Wall Street brokerage and investment banking firm, died Wednesday 
at Greenwich Hospital in Connecticut, where he was being treated for 
injuries after a recent automobile accident. He was 90 and had lived 
in Greenwich for 68 years. Lasell was associated with Morgan Stanley 
for 60 years. He was president from 1971 until he retired in 1973. 
After graduating from Williams College in 1930, he worked for J.P. 
Morgan &AMP; Co. and Price Waterhouse &AMP; Co. He joined Morgan Stanley 
when it was founded in 1935 and became a partner in 1951. Until recently 
he was an advisory director. He was born in Whitinsville, Mass., and 
graduated from the Pomfret School in Pomfret, Conn. He was a former 
president of the school's trustees and former executive committee 
member of the Williams College Society of Alumni. During World War 
II, he served in the Naval Reserve, retiring as lieutenant commander 
in 1946. His first wife, Ruth Drake Lasell, died in 1973. Survivors 
include his wife, Elizabeth, of Greenwich; a daughter, Virginia Westgaard 
of Denver; a son, Chester of Manchester, Vt., and Vero Beach, Fla.; 
a stepdaughter, Elizabeth Cousineau of Seattle; six grandchildren 
and two great-grandchildren.
