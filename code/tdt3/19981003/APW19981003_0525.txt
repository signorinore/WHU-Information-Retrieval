A lock of King Louis XVI's gray hair, shorn minutes after the monarch 
was beheaded during the French Revolution 205 years ago, was auctioned 
off Saturday for 31,000 francs (dlrs 5,536). A French collector who 
asked not to be identified by name acquired the hair, encased in a 
period frame decorated with a gold coin and three ``fleurs de lys,'' 
the symbol of French royalty. ``It's for a royalist friend of mine 
who had heard about this relic,'' said the buyer, refusing to elaborate. 
Several telephone bids were taken during the auction, over in three 
minutes. Bidding for, 3rd graf pvs (eg-parf)
