Several Florence museums, including the Uffizi Gallery, will be closed 
for at least several hours on Tuesday because of a meeting here between 
the French and Italian premiers and other top officials. The city's 
art superintendent, Antonio Paolucci, said the Uffizi will be closed 
from 8:30 a.m. to noon. The museum is next to the Palazzo Vecchio, 
site of the closing news conference by the politicians, and is connected 
to Palazzo Pitti, which will host most of the talks. After opening 
at noon, the Uffizi will then stay open till 10 p.m. Among the tourists 
spots closed because of the talks will be the Boboli Gardens across 
the Arno river, from 8:30 a.m. till 1 p.m. on Tuesday. Coming from 
Paris for the periodic consultations between France and Italy will 
be French President Jacques Chirac and Premier Lionel Jospin as well 
as several ministers. The talks start Monday in the Palazzo Pitti.
