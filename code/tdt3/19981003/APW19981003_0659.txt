Americans Lars Jorgensen and Wendy Ingraham set men's and women's 
course records Saturday in 3.9-kilometer (2.4-mile) swim segment of 
the Hawaiian Triathlon World Championship. Jorgensen surged to a lead 
of better than 100 meters early and was timed in 46 minutes, 41 seconds, 
winning by nearly two-minutes. He broke the record of 46:44 he set 
in 1995. Ingraham, who finished fourth among women last year, completed 
the swim in 12th place overall in 49:09. She also broke a record she 
had set, 49:52 she set last year. A record field of 1,524 professional 
and age-group amateurs were registered for the 20th anniversary year 
of the 140.6-mile three-sport event. The 180-kilometer (112-mile) 
cycling and 42.195-kilometer (26.2-mile) marathon segments followed.
