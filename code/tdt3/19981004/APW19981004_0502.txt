Blyth Tait won the individual gold medal and led the New Zealand team 
to the victory in the three-day event at the 1998 World Equestrian 
Games Sunday. Fellow New Zealander Mark Todd took the silver on Broadcast 
News, while Sweden's Paula Tornquist on SAS Monaghan finished third. 
Defending champion Vaughn Jeffreys, also from New Zealand, was fourth. 
After the event's final test, show jumping, Tait, on Ready Teddy, 
had 43.4 penalties, compared to Todd's 44.2. Tornquist finished with 
45.6 penalties. David O'Connor of The Plains, Va. scored the U.S. 
team best result to finish sixth on Giltedge with 51.4 penalties. 
Kerry Millikin of Westport, Mass., placed 15 on Out and About, and 
two-time World champion Bruce Davidson of Unionsville, Pa. finished 
21th on Heyday. At the three-day event team competition, New Zealand 
claimed the gold with a total of 137.6 penalties, while France finished 
second with 182.8, and Britain took the bronze with 184.9. The Americans, 
finished fourth with a total of 186.1 penalties. At the Atlanta Olympics, 
Australia won, while the Americans claimed the silver and New Zealand 
took the bronze. The three-day event was held in Pratoni del Vivaro, 
50 kilometers (30 miles) southeast of Rome, at a track inaugurated 
during the Rome 1960 Olympic Games in the capital. In the individual 
dressage, scheduled to finish later Sunday with the event's final 
test, the Free Style, Isabel Werth of Germany was atop the provisional 
standings.
