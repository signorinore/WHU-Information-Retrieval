On a seaside beach packed with pilgrims, Pope John Paul II sought 
Sunday to heal the wounds left by war and totalitarian rule and urged 
the international community to provide ``timely help'' to end the 
Kosovo conflict. In his departure remarks, with President Tudjman 
with him at the airport, the pope said Croatia ``must show great patience, 
wisdom, a willingness to make sacrifices and generous solidarity in 
order to overcome definitively the present postwar phase and attain 
the noble goals to which she aspires.'' ``Democracy comes at a high 
price; the money to pay for it is minted from the noble metal of honesty, 
reasonableness, respect for others, the spirit of sacrifice, and patience. 
To claim that it can be bought with a different currency is to run 
the risk of bankruptcy.'' Tudjman is often criticized by western governments 
for authoritarianism. John Paul arrived back in Rome late Sunday evening
