Defending champion Isaac Chemobo bested the course record by a whopping 
one minute and 41 seconds Sunday as he won the men's 30-kilometer 
race in Lidingoloppet, the world's biggest cross-country festival. 
Chemobo's winning time was 1 hour, 33 minutes and 33 seconds. Fellow 
Kenyan William Musyoki set the old course record in 1996. Philip Rugut 
and Wilson Omwoyo made it a podium sweep for Kenya, finishing in 1:34.27 
and 1:36.59 respectively. Portugal's Joao Lopes placed fourth in 1:37.23. 
Former world marathon champion Douglas Wakiihuri of Kenya was 11th. 
Finland's Annemarie Sandell, who finished fifth in the recent World 
Half Marathon Championships, won the women's 15K for the fourth time 
in 53.52. More than 23,000 runners competed in the two-day festival 
on Lidingo Island outside Stockholm.
