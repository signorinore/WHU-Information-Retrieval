BRUSSELS, Belgium (AP) _ NATO Secretary-General Javier Solana said 
Monday that Yugoslavia had not yet complied with United Nations resolutions 
on Kosovo and stressed that NATO was ready to launch a military attack. 
Supreme Allied Commander Europe Wesley K. Clark said that, once given 
the green light, NATO's military operation would likely be fast and 
furious. ``NATO action, should a political decison be taken, could 
come in a matter of hours and days,'' Clark told reporters. Solana 
went beyond the assessment of U.N. Secretary-General Kofi Annan, who 
said in a report in New York that he lacked the means to verify whether 
Yugoslav President Slobodan Milosevic had complied with U.N. demands 
aimed at halting the conflict. ``I'm sure that Mr. Annan has put in 
his document what he thinks is the reality. The reality according 
to us is that the compliance has not taken place,'' said Solana. Solana 
had already met earlier Monday with U.S. special envoy Richard Holbrooke, 
who said the military situation in Kosovo remains critical despite 
a lull in fighting and the removal of some Yugoslav tanks and troops 
from the battlefield. After a meeting with Solana and Clark, Holbrooke
