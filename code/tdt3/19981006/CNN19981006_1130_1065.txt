Investors were comforted by a surprise earnings report from Motorola. 
The world's largest maker of phones, pagers and Telecom equipment 
posted a better-than-expected quarterly profit. Still, Motorola's 
earnings are down 13% from the same period last year. To boost its 
profits, Motorola set a restructuring program back in June, which 
included laying off 10% of its work force, or 15,000 workers. Shares 
of Motorola jumping nearly 4.5 at 43.
