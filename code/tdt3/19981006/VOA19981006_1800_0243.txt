Russia's government is appealing for calm Wednesday. during a day 
of nationwide strikes and rallies. VOA'S Peter Heimlein in Moscow 
reports, tens of millions of people are expected to take part in the 
protests. As communist and trade union leaders predicted, the biggest 
anti-government demonstration since the collapse of the Soviet Union, 
Justice Minister Pavel Kresheninikof urged restraint. He said, I appeal 
to protesters to be calm and tolerant of opposing views. Protests 
are scheduled in cities and towns all across Russia. Communist leader 
Genati Zyrganof says all gatherings will be asked to adopt resolutions 
calling for President Boris Yeltsin's resignation. The largest demonstration 
is expected in Moscow where as many as 150,000 people are expected 
to march on the Kremlin for a rally outside Red Square. Moscow police 
say they will have 11,000 officers on duty to keep order at the Kremlin 
rally. Peter Heimlein, VOA News, Moscow.
