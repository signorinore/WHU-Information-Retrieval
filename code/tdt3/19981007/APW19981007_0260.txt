Barcelona's 92,000-capacity Nou Camp stadium will stage the final 
of the European Champions' League on May 26, UEFA announced Wednesday. 
Villa Park, home of English Premier League club Aston Villa, will 
host the May 19 final of the Cupwinner's Cup, while Spartak Moscow's 
84,000-seat Luzhniki stadium will be the venue for the May 12 final 
of the UEFA Cup, European soccer's governing body said after a two-day 
meeting here. Nou Camp and Luzhniki were among the 12 five-star venues 
listed by UEFA as possible venues for the final of its flagship competition. 
Villa Park, which has a capacity of 39,339, was classed as a four-star 
venue.
