Singapore banks may be ready for their second round of rate slashing 
in a month after United Overseas Bank set the pace Wednesday with

cut from 7.25 to 7 percent will be effective Thursday, the bank said. 
``There is a lot more room for the prime rate to go down,'' Lim Beng 
Eu, an analyst at Vickers Ballas Investment Research, told Dow Jones 
Newswires. Intensified global pressure for lower interest rates, less 
domestic demand for loans and falling as Bank's move soon.
