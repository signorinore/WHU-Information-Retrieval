Teruo Sugihara shot a 2-under-par 70 Thursday and took a one-stroke 
first-round lead in the 50 million yen (dlrs 407,000) Japan Senior 
Open golf championship. The 61-year-old Sugihara, seeking his first 
victory this season and his seventh overall in Japan PGA senior tournaments, 
shot two birdies on the 6,749-yard, par-72 Shimoakima Country Club 
course, northwest of Tokyo. Fujio Kobayashi and Akira Kawamata shot 
71s. Taiwanese golfers Hsieh Min-nan and Kuo Chie-hsiung shared fourth 
place with Haruo Yasuda of Japan at 72. A total of 126 players, including 
five non-Japanese, teed off in clear weather. Terry Gale of Australia 
shot 75, Chen Ching-po of Taiwan 79 and Arjin Sophon of Thailand 80. 
The winner will collect 7.5 million yen (dlrs 61,000).
