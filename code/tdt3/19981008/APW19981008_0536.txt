Second-year pro Takeo Nogami fired a 4-under-par 68 Thursday, taking 
a one-stroke first-round lead in the 110 million yen (dlrs 894,000) 
Tokai Classic. The 27-year-old Nogami, seeking his first victory on 
the pro tour, birdied the first two holes and added three more later, 
against one bogey, on the 7,060-yard, par-72 Miyoshi Country Club 
course in central Japan. Kaname Yokoo and Nobumitsu Yuhara both shot 
69. Shigeki Maruyama, seeking his second PGA Tour victory this season, 
shot a 70 and was tied for fourth with three other Japanese _ Tsuyoshi 
Yoneyama, Hideki Kase and Masao Suzuki. At 71, British Open runner-up 
Brian Watts of the United States shared eighth place with Taiwan's 
Yeh Chang-ting and three Japanese _ Shinichi Yokota, Koki Idoki and 
Yasunori Ida. Hidemichi Tanaka, who won the Japan Open last week, 
shot a 73 and shared 18th place with Mark Calcavecchia of the United 
States and 10 other Japanese. A total of 120 players, including 18 
non-Japanese, teed off in clear weather. The winner will receive 19.8 
million yen (dlrs 161,000).
